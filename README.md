# Sprint-Review-and-Retrospective

# How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
Getting to know *why* a user needs something is very important to the solution of the problem and the pursuit of efficiency. User stories give a good perspective on why a user might want a change, therefore making it easier to satisfy the request.

# How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
I prefer to take projects bit by bit, it is important to not overwhelm yourself and lose track of what's important. I plan to incorporate daily standups for team alignment, sprint planning to set achievable goals, retrospectives to honestly reflect on what went wrong and right, and Kanban or Scrum boards to visualize workflow.

# What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
Good communication is key for a good team member. Showing up to meeting, keeping your team up-to-date on what you've gotten done and what you're going to do, and talking about what might be slowing you down are extremely useful in the long run. Good communication in a team can save days of time possibly wasted on things that don't matter or things that are keeping the project held back.
